Childhood

Ryan Thomas Gosling was born on November 12, 1980, in London, Ontario, Canada. His mother worked as a secretary, and his father was a salesman. His parents divorced when Ryan was 13 years old. He was primarily raised by his mother and older sister.

He suffered from dyslexia (difficulty with reading) since childhood, which made it difficult for him to do well in school. However, he showed an early interest in the stage and music. Inspired by the movie "Rambo," he even brought a knife to school once, for which he was suspended for a year.

Early career

At the age of 12, he auditioned for The Mickey Mouse Club (1993-1995), where he performed alongside future stars such as Britney Spears, Christina Aguilera, and Justin Timberlake. This marked his first significant step into the world of show business.

After the show, he began acting in Canadian and American TV series:

  • Are You Afraid of the Dark?
  • Goosebumps
  • Breaker High

He got his first serious film role in 2001 in the film The Believer, where he played a Jewish neo-Nazi. The role earned him critical acclaim.

Breakthrough and success

He gained worldwide fame after the film "The Notebook" (2004). His on-screen relationship with Rachel McAdams became iconic.

Since then, Gosling has appeared in several successful films:

  • Half Nelson
  • Drive
  • Blade Runner 2049
  • Barbie
  • Blue Valentine

He also tried his hand at directing, with "Lost River" (2014) being his debut film.

Personal life

Since 2011, Ryan has been in a relationship with actress Eva Mendes . They have two daughters and prefer to keep their personal lives private.

Gosling is also involved in music, having founded the band Dead Man's Bones, where he sings and plays guitar and piano. He is known for his humility, sense of style, and avoidance of social media.
